Hi, my name is Jesse.

I am a 31-year-old hobbyist who has been enjoying working with images since I was 11 years old. My curiosity of image design originates from the early 2000s of the web, where I spotted interesting graphical signatures being displayed on website forums. My first professional application used at the time was Adobe Photoshop 7. Over multiple years, I would also take an interest in web design and designing simple templates and splicing them.

At the age of 17, I had a friend who asked me if I could edit her photos in Photoshop. While I never really edited photography, this was a massive discovery that my post-processing experience in Photoshop could translate to photo retouch. For the next few months and onward, I would delve into the image correction world. I began to find a lot of enjoyment retouching photos and took any opportunity to edit when I could.

But then I thought to myself: why don’t I just get the images myself? This was the pivotal moment where I decided that I wanted to purchase a DSLR for myself. I would save up for almost a year at my part-time job at Best Buy, in hopes to buying a proper camera for myself. Then, in December of 2011, I bought my first DSLR ever: the Canon T3i.

I would hit the ground running, learning every facet of photography that I could. I was slowly convincing myself that photography could be a career for myself. In 2013, I enrolled myself into a B.F.A. of Photography at Barry University, a private liberal arts school located in Miami. At this point in time, photography became my entire life. After three semesters of my Photography degree, I realized that things just did not feel right anymore and withdrew from Barry.

Pursuing a possible career in photography is what taught me that it should just be a passionate hobby of mine, instead of a career. Most recently, returning to shooting photography in my free time has reinvigorated my passion to being behind the lens.

Photography is a beautiful form of art that allows a person to capture a specific moment in time, where it can be timelessly archived. And that’s what I’m here to do – documenting unforgettable memories.

Thank you for visiting my site.
